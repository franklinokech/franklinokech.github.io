About Me

Hi, I'm Franklin Okech, and I'm passionate about writing clean, efficiently designed code. As a developer for analytics and data science, I aim to build scalable, fault-tolerant, low-latency systems.

I wrote my first line of code around age 19 and, looking back, can only wish that eureka moment had occured five years earlier. The boundless world of code is what keeps me up at night and keeps me constantly occupied. I primarily know and use Python most heavily, but have dabbled in some PHP, Java, and Javascript. With Python, I've built production systems using everything from its scientific stack, to its standard library on an airgapped computer, to Django.

I currently work in Komaza a forestry company in Kilifi, Kenya., doing full-stack development of their analytics-focused harvest department. One ubiquitous theme has been driving big efficiency improvements everywhere possible. I'm still going strong on my New Year's resolution of contributing more to open-source code.

I learn through teaching, and writing for franky data tips has been a great way to express that. In addition to writing for the site, I've provided detailed technical reviews for dozens of articles there as well. You can also find me on Stack Overflow, where I'm constantly trying to answer more questions than I ask, and on github.
